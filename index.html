Welcome to the new
Agroforestry Hub website

New content, new aims, new look!

The Agroforestry Hub will grow over the next few weeks into a hub for all things agroforestry in the UK and Europe. Along with a new design there will be a range of new features rolled out.

To start with, a section describing the basics of agroforestry will be a one-stop-shop for the agroforestry basics, whatever your background. The news section will provide topical information, project updates, policy changes, and anything else of interest. The agroforestry map features a range of agroforestry farms, consultants, and associated organisations.

We're also branching out to new areas, such as the projects section which will feature the latest farmer-led and student-led research projects. We hope this will allow greater integration of research into practical agroforestry examples on farms. As ever, we will also have links to relevent organisation who may be able to provide specfic support for your region and style of agroforestry.

Ultimately we want this to be a community for agroforestry in the UK and Europe. Farmers, researchers, industry leaders, producers, marketers, and many more all have a role to play in developing agroforestry in the UK and Europe. We hope that the Agroforestry Hub can be a basis for driving agroforestry forward.

On that note, if you have any ideas or projects you would like to be included, send us an email! Or stay up to date and in the know by signing up for a Membership

What we're all about

For all people

The Agroforestry Hub is the place for those interested in agroforestry, whether you're a farmer, researcher, student, industry representative, marketer, or just curious.

The root of information

This is a singular location for all your agroforestry information for the UK, including news, webinars, farm walks, relevent articles and much more.

Pinpoint agroforestry near you

Seeing is believing, so find out where the nearest agroforestry farm is to you and get in contact or look out for open days and farm walks.

Sharing is caring

Find links to other organisations relevent to agroforestry around the UK, Ireland and Europe. Also, explore the database of student and farmer agroforestry projects
